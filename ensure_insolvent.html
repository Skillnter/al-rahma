The Health

The health sector in the Sultanate, through its institutions spread throughout the Sultanate, succeeded in providing all basic health care services to the Omani citizen and the resident, and this development had a positive reflection on all health indicators according to which the Sultanate became classified among the developed countries in the field of providing health services at the world level .

The sound and continuous planning that the Sultanate pursues through health development plans, which are implemented successively every five years, starting in the year 1976 AD, has supported the health system by providing health programs and services that contributed to raising the general health level of the population.

The year 2013 is the third year for the implementation of the eighth five-year plan for health development (2011-2015), which is a continuation of a new method in planning followed since the seventh five-year plan, which is the methodology of “strategic planning”, which led to providing a clear vision and sound orientation in order to identify and address problems And priority needs.

The “Health Outlook 2050 خطة” health plan, which is a long-term plan, represents a transitional phase of the health system to keep pace with the medical and technical development in the health system worldwide. The health care system consists of three integrated levels:

Effective and high-quality primary health care provided by local centers, health complexes and hospitals covering all governorates and states of the Sultanate.

Then the second level, which is secondary health care provided by reference hospitals located in all governorates of the Sultanate and state hospitals located in some of the major states of the Sultanate that provide medical care for specialized health problems, and provide more skilled and specialized care.

Then the third level, which is the high-tech specialized healthcare, which is provided by the major hospitals in the Governorate of Muscat, which are the Royal Hospital, Khawla Hospital, and Al Nahda Hospital, which are all hospitals of a comprehensive nature that work as reference hospitals for all parts of the Sultanate. Al-Masara Hospital in Muscat Governorate is considered a specialized hospital on the third level for mental and neurological diseases.

The number of hospitals in the Sultanate has reached (65) hospitals, of which (49) are hospitals affiliated with the Ministry of Health, and (5) are hospitals affiliated with government agencies such as Sultan Qaboos University and Royal Oman Police and medical services for the Sultan Armed Forces.

By the end of the year (2012), the total government primary health care institutions in the Sultanate reached (220) health centers, complexes and a local hospital, of which (219) are managed by the Ministry of Health and include (69) health centers in a family, (99) health centers without a family, and (22) (Health complex), and (29) local hospitals.

The private health sector is an additional tributary to the health services in the Sultanate and is growing steadily, as the number of private hospitals (10) hospitals, the number of public clinics (430) public clinics, and (224) specialist clinics and health complex, as well as (187) dental clinics, in addition to ( 59) a clinic specializing in Chinese and Indian medicine, (3) medical laboratory centers, and (8) MRI diagnostic centers. The number of private pharmacies reached (442) pharmacies in 2011.
